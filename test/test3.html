Rice Production

Rice cultivation is an ancient art perfected over millennia. The process begins with careful seed selection and land preparation, followed by flooding of paddy fields to create ideal growing conditions.

Indian farmers employ both traditional and modern techniques, nurturing rice plants through monsoon seasons. The flooded fields not only provide water but also control weeds and pests naturally.

Rice Processing

After harvest, rice undergoes meticulous processing. The journey begins with drying to reduce moisture content, ensuring long-term storage quality.

Milling removes the outer husk and bran layers. For white rice, both are removed; brown rice retains the nutritious bran layer.

Types of Rice

Basmati Rice

Known for its distinctive aroma and long grains. Perfect for biryanis and pilafs.

  • • Aromatic fragrance
  • • Fluffy texture
  • • Low glycemic index

Steam Rice

Parboiled rice that retains more nutrients. Ideal for everyday firm meals.

  • • Enhanced nutrition
  • • Separate grains
  • • Quick cooking

Brown Rice

Whole grain rice with bran layer intact. Superior nutritional value and fiber.

  • • High fiber content
  • • Nutty flavor
  • • Whole grain benefits
